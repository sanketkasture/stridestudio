What do we offer?

At Stride, each class is a journey inward - to build resilience, presence, and peace. Whether you are a beginner or experienced, every Stride counts. Stride isn't just a studio - it's a sanctuary for your well being.

Iyengar Yoga

Developed by B.K.S Iyengar, it is a form of yoga that emphasizes precision, alignment, and the use of props to achieve stability and depth in each posture.

Matwork Pilates

Developed by Joseph Pilates, it is a low-impact exercise method focused on core strength, flexibility, and controlled movement using just your body and a mat.

Restorative Yoga

Unwind and recharge with our Restorative Yoga classes. Our classes also incorporate Pranayama (breathwork) and meditation techniques to quiet the mind, reduce stress, and cultivate inner peace.

FAQ

Answers to Common Yoga and Meditation Questions

1. How is Yoga different from Pilates?
Yoga focuses on holding poses, breathing techniques, and meditation to unite body, mind, and spirit. Pilates emphasizes core strength, body control, and precise movements to improve posture, balance, and overall physical fitness.

2. Can I practice both Yoga and Pilates?
Yes, many people find that combining both practices enhances their overall well-being and fitness.

3. Do I need prior experience?
No, classes cater to all levels.

4. Suitable for beginners?
Yes, both practices are great introductions.

5. How often to attend?
2-3 times a week for noticeable results.

6. Benefits
Improved strength, flexibility, balance, and reduced stress.

7. What should I do if I have pain during a class?
If you experience pain, stop and modify the pose/exercise or rest. Listen to your body and avoid pushing past pain.

8. Can these practices help me stay calm and happy?
Yes, both yoga and Pilates can help reduce stress and anxiety, promoting calmness and happiness.

1. Safe for injuries/chronic conditions?
Yes, with guidance from our experienced teacher who will provide modifications and adjustments to accommodate your needs.

2. Help with back pain/posture/stress?
Yes, both practices can help alleviate these issues.

3. Are these practices safe for back pain or knee pain?
Our experienced teacher will provide personalized guidance, modifications, and adjustments to help you practice safely with back or knee pain.

4. Is it okay if my body feels painful or tired after class?
Some discomfort is normal, especially if you're new. However, sharp pain or excessive fatigue may indicate overexertion. Listen to your body and adjust your practice accordingly.

5. Inform teacher about medical history?
Yes, essential for safety.
